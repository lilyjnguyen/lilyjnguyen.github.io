This is the website promoting my senior show called Nurture, with its opening reception on April 1, 2025.
And no, that is not a joke. But perhaps it’s very fitting that this exploration of plants, nature, and our
increasingly distanced relationship with the earth is happening on April Fool’s Day. It’s almost like the
universe is laughing at us for thinking we can solve our deeply ingrained ecological disconnect by staring
at some plants in a gallery. But we’ll get there.

Nurture is a show that explores the tension between creation and destruction,
the fragility of identity, trying to decipher between the real and the faux,
and the absurdity of trying to make sense of it all in an increasingly fractured
world, all through the lens of our relationship to plants and the environment in
which we live. Through mixed-media installations, sculptures, and art that definitely
took more time than it should have to finish, I invite you to reflect on the paradox of
nurturing: how can we care for others when we are still trying to figure out how to care
for ourselves? Is it even possible to "nurture" anything anymore?

Yes, this all sounds very deep. But don’t worry, there’s irony here too. If you’re expecting
some grand existential breakthrough that will change your life or give you the answers to the
universe—sorry to disappoint. I’m just as confused as you are, and I’ve been to art school.
This show isn’t about making sense of anything; it’s about not making sense, which I think is
much more relatable these days. But underneath it all, there’s a thread of hope—one that comes
from acknowledging the messiness of our human condition, yet we still attempt to nurture something.
Anything.

So please, come for the existential crises, stay for the painfully aware commentary on my internal
struggles with my own relationship to nature. And of course, the art. You might think some of it’s good.
Maybe.
